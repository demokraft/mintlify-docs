The DemoKraft AI Slack integration keeps your sales team informed the moment a high-intent event happens in an AI Hub session. Instead of checking dashboards or waiting for CRM updates, your team receives a structured alert card in the Slack channel of your choice — with the visitor's name, company, qualification score, content watched, questions asked, and direct links to their Lead Intelligence profile and CRM record.

## What it sends

The Slack bot posts an alert when any of the following events occur:

- A session completion score exceeds your configured hot lead threshold (default: 75)
- A visitor books a meeting via Calendly during a session
- A visitor starts a free trial from a session CTA
- A visitor from a tracked company domain completes a session

## Setup

<Steps>
  <Step title="Connect Slack">
    Go to **Settings → Integrations → Slack** and click **Connect**. Complete the OAuth flow and select the Slack workspace you want to connect. You are returned to DemoKraft AI once the connection is authorised.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select your notification channel">
    In the Slack integration settings, click **Select Channel** and choose the channel where alerts should be posted — for example, `#sales-alerts` or `#hot-leads`. Click **Save**.

    <Note>
      To post alerts to a private channel, invite the DemoKraft AI bot first. Open the channel in Slack and type `/invite @demoKraft AI`, then return to settings and select the channel.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure your alert triggers">
    Click **Configure Alerts** and set your preferences:

    | Alert | Default |
    |---|---|
    | Hot lead completed session (score ≥ threshold) | On — threshold: 75 |
    | Meeting Booked | On |
    | Trial Started | On |
    | Company Domain Alert | Off — configure domains to enable |
    | Session Completed (all sessions) | Off |

    Adjust the hot lead score threshold to match your team's definition of a priority lead. Lower it to see more alerts; raise it to focus only on the highest-scoring sessions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What an alert looks like

When a hot lead alert fires, Slack displays a card with a structured summary of the session:

```
🔥 Hot Lead — DemoKraft AI Hub
Visitor: Sarah Chen (sarah@acmecorp.com)
Company: Acme Corp | Score: 87/100
Watched: Product Demo (94%), CRM Integration (78%)
Asked: "Does this work with Salesforce Enterprise?"
Outcome: Meeting booked — Jun 7, 2026 at 10:00am
→ View full profile  →  Open in HubSpot
```

The alert includes direct links to the Lead Intelligence profile in DemoKraft AI and to the contact record in your connected CRM.

## Per-session alert routing

For account-based campaigns, you can route alerts for a specific session to a different Slack channel — for example, sending alerts directly to the account executive responsible for that account.

To configure per-session routing:

1. Open the AI Hub session.
2. Go to **Session Settings → Alerts**.
3. Select **Override channel** and choose the target Slack channel.
4. Optionally, set a different score threshold for this session.

<Tip>
  Use per-session routing to send enterprise account alerts to a dedicated `#enterprise-alerts` channel and route SMB sessions to a separate channel — giving each team only the signals relevant to them.
</Tip>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Bot is not posting alerts">
    The most common cause is that the bot is not a member of the channel. For private channels, open the channel in Slack and type `/invite @demoKraft AI`. For public channels, check that the correct channel is selected in **Settings → Integrations → Slack**.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Channel not found when selecting in settings">
    The channel must exist and the DemoKraft AI bot must be a member before it appears in the channel dropdown. Create the channel first, invite the bot, then return to settings to select it.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Alerts are missing for some sessions">
    Check that the session's qualification score met your configured threshold. If a session scored below the threshold, no alert fires. Lower the threshold in **Configure Alerts** if you want to capture more sessions, or enable the **Session Completed** trigger to receive alerts for all sessions regardless of score.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Bot was removed from the channel">
    Re-invite the bot to the channel using `/invite @demoKraft AI`. Confirm that alerts resume by running a test session. If the bot was removed from the workspace entirely, reconnect the integration via **Settings → Integrations → Slack → Reconnect**.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
