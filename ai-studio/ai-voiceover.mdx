Once you approve your script, AI Voiceover takes over and replaces your original audio track — background noise, filler words, inconsistent pacing and all — with a clean, professional AI-synthesised narration. The voiceover auto-synchronises to your video timeline so every caption, visual effect, and on-screen action lines up precisely. You preview the full result before anything is finalised, and you can fine-tune individual segments without regenerating the entire track.

## Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select a voice">
    Open the **Voiceover** panel in the editor. Filter the voice library by **language**, **gender**, **accent**, and **style**. Click **Preview** on any voice to hear a 15-second sample narrated with your own script text, then click **Select** to apply it to your project.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate the voiceover">
    Click **Generate Voiceover**. AI Studio synthesises the full narration and aligns it to the video timeline automatically. Generation typically takes 1–3 minutes for a 5-minute video. A preview plays automatically when it completes — listen through the full video before finalising.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fine-tune sync">
    If any segment sounds slightly out of sync with the on-screen action, click that segment in the timeline and drag the **Trim** handles to adjust its start and end point. Click **Re-sync** to let the AI re-align that segment to the updated timing without touching the rest of the track.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fix pronunciations">
    Click the **Pronunciations** button in the Voiceover panel. Add a phonetic spelling for any product name, technical term, or acronym the AI mispronounces — for example, _"DemoKraft"_ → _"DEH-moh-kraft"_. Then click **Re-generate Segment** to apply the correction to just that sentence. Pronunciation entries are saved workspace-wide and applied automatically in all future projects.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add background music (optional)">
    Open the **Audio** panel and click **Add Background Music**. Choose a track from the built-in royalty-free library. Filter by mood: **Neutral**, **Upbeat**, **Cinematic**, or **Minimal**. Set the volume slider to 15–25% of the voiceover volume — the recommended level for B2B content — and enable **Auto-fade** to automatically lower the music whenever someone is speaking.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Voice Consistency Across Projects

Keep all your workspace content sounding like one brand by setting a default voice:

1. Go to **Settings → Brand Kit**.
2. Under **Default Voice**, select your preferred voice.
3. All new projects will open with this voice pre-selected. You can override it per project at any time.

<Tip>
  Set a Default Voice in your Brand Kit to maintain a consistent brand experience across your entire product demo library, onboarding content, and knowledge base — without having to re-select it for every new project.
</Tip>
